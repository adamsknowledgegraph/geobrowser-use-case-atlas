Why knowledge graphs

Knowledge graphs empower provenance, verifiability, and context.

Instead of losing information inside isolated pages, databases, PDFs, and apps, a knowledge graph keeps people, places, claims, sources, studies, events, and topics connected. That makes simple but important questions easier to answer: who said what, what is the context, and is it verified?

01

Trace

Who said what?

Follow a claim back to the source, see the surrounding context, and check whether it is supported or verified.

02

Participate

Can people improve it together?

Members can add sources, challenge claims, refine entities, and keep shared knowledge up to date in public view.

03

Build

Can it become a useful product?

Once the data is connected, the same graph can power explainers, maps, dossiers, search tools, and consumer apps.
